---
title: "Adjust Antigravity CLI settings and color schemes"
description: "Use /settings to open the settings panel, choose a color scheme, and manage credentials. Changes sync bidirectionally with the Antigravity 2.0 GUI automatically."
---

Antigravity CLI gives you direct control over your preferences through the `/settings` command. Changes you make here sync bidirectionally with Antigravity 2.0, so you never have to configure the same option twice.

## Open and change settings

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open settings">
    Type `/settings` in the CLI prompt and press Enter. The **Settings** panel opens in the terminal.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select a color scheme">
    Navigate to the **Color scheme** option. The **terminal** color scheme appears at the top of the list and is the default choice. Select the scheme that fits your environment and confirm.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save and exit">
    Your selection is applied immediately. Close the **Settings** panel to return to your session.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Sign out and clear credentials

Run `/logout` to sign out of your account and remove saved credentials from the system keyring. You will be prompted to authenticate again the next time you start a session.

## Settings sync with Antigravity 2.0

<Note>
  Preferences and permissions sync bidirectionally between the CLI and the Antigravity 2.0 GUI. A change you make in one interface is reflected in the other automatically — no manual re-configuration required.
</Note>
